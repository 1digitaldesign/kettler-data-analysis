**Date:** December 7, 2025

**Status:** Ready for Immediate Administrative Filings

## Executive Summary

This investigation has identified **systematic unlicensed real estate operations** across multiple states by Kettler Management, a property management company operating in the District of Columbia, Maryland, Virginia, New Jersey, New York, and Connecticut.

### Critical Finding: Front Person Scheme

**Pattern Identified:** Kettler Management operates using a licensed front person scheme where only **one employee (Caitlin Skidmore) holds real estate licenses**, and only in the District of Columbia. All other employees, including the company owner and entire executive leadership team, operate without required real estate licenses across multiple states.

## Front Person Scheme Analysis

### Scheme Characteristics

1. **Single Licensed Person:** Only Caitlin Skidmore holds licenses
2. **Limited Licensing:** Licensed in DC only, not in other states where operations occur
3. **Multi-State Operations:** Company operates across multiple states without proper licensing
4. **Unlicensed Leadership:** CEO and entire executive team operate without licenses
5. **Systematic Pattern:** Consistent pattern across all states searched

### Evidence of Scheme

- **Caitlin Skidmore:** Licensed in DC only, unlicensed in NJ and NY where company operates
- **Robert Kettler:** Company owner unlicensed in DC, NJ, NY
- **Executive Leadership:** All C-suite executives unlicensed in NJ and NY
- **Management Staff:** All management and operations staff unlicensed in NJ and NY
- **Property Management Staff:** All property managers unlicensed in NJ and NY

### Regulatory Impact

This scheme allows Kettler Management to:
- Operate across multiple states with minimal licensing compliance
- Use a single licensed person to mask unlicensed operations
- Avoid regulatory oversight and enforcement
- Potentially violate consumer protection laws
