# Why Kettler and Related Companies Are Registered in Texas

**Date:** December 11, 2025
**Analysis:** Texas Incorporation Strategy and Shell Company Patterns

## Executive Summary

Kettler and many related companies are registered in Texas for several strategic reasons:
1. **Business-friendly incorporation laws** (similar to Delaware/Nevada)
2. **Lariat Realty Advisors base of operations** (Frisco, TX)
3. **Shell company structure** (Frisco address cluster)
4. **Privacy and regulatory advantages**
5. **Cost-effective business registration**

### 3. Texas Business Advantages

#### A. Business-Friendly Incorporation Laws
- **No state corporate income tax** (for most entities)
- **Low filing fees** compared to other states
- **Flexible corporate structure** options
- **Strong asset protection** laws
- **Privacy protections** for business owners

#### B. Regulatory Advantages
- **Minimal reporting requirements** (compared to Delaware)
- **No annual franchise tax** for many entity types
- **Flexible management structures**
- **Strong LLC protections**

#### C. Strategic Location
- **Central location** for multi-state operations
- **Business-friendly courts** and legal system
- **Established business infrastructure**
- **Access to major markets** (Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex)

### 4. Shell Company Structure Benefits

**Why Use Texas for Shell Companies:**

1. **Privacy:**
   - Texas allows use of registered agent services
   - Business addresses can be separate from operations
   - Owner information not always publicly disclosed

2. **Cost Efficiency:**
   - Lower incorporation costs than Delaware
   - Minimal ongoing compliance costs
   - Shared addresses reduce overhead

3. **Regulatory Arbitrage:**
   - Operate in other states while incorporated in Texas
   - Avoid stricter regulations in operational states
   - Use Texas entity structure for multi-state operations

4. **Asset Protection:**
   - Texas LLC laws provide strong asset protection
   - Separation of entities for liability protection
   - Multi-entity structure for risk management

## Regulatory Implications

### Why This Matters:

1. **Foreign Qualification Requirements:**
   - Companies incorporated in Texas but operating in other states must qualify as foreign entities
   - Our analysis shows **Kettler may not be properly qualified** in operational states
   - This is a **regulatory violation**

2. **Shell Company Concerns:**
   - Multiple companies at same address suggests shell company structure
   - May be used to obscure true ownership
   - Could indicate fraudulent or deceptive practices

3. **Broker Supervision Issues:**
   - One broker (Caitlin Skidmore) serving multiple companies
   - May violate supervision requirements
   - Questions about proper oversight

4. **Privacy vs. Transparency:**
   - Texas incorporation provides privacy
   - But may hide beneficial ownership
   - Regulatory agencies may have difficulty tracking ownership

## Comparison: Texas vs. Other States

### Texas vs. Delaware
- **Delaware:** More expensive, more reporting, stronger case law
- **Texas:** Less expensive, less reporting, business-friendly
- **Choice:** Companies choose Texas for cost savings

### Texas vs. Nevada
- **Nevada:** Strong privacy, no information sharing
- **Texas:** Good privacy, but more transparent than Nevada
- **Choice:** Balance between privacy and legitimacy

### Texas vs. Operational States (VA, MD, DC)
- **Operational States:** Stricter regulations, higher costs
- **Texas:** More flexible, lower costs
- **Choice:** Incorporate in Texas, operate elsewhere
